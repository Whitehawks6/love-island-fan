1950 – 1974

The Dream

From Turing's imitation game to the first neural networks. A time of boundless optimism where machines were taught to play checkers and speak simple English.

1950

The Turing Test

Alan Turing publishes 'Computing Machinery and Intelligence', proposing the 'Imitation Game' to determine if a machine can demonstrate human-like intelligence.

1956

Dartmouth Workshop

The term 'Artificial Intelligence' is coined. John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, and others gather for a summer workshop that marks the official birth of AI as a field.

Read more
1958

Perceptron

Frank Rosenblatt invents the Perceptron, an electronic device that showed the ability to learn. It was the ancestor of modern neural networks.

Read more
1966

ELIZA

Joseph Weizenbaum creates ELIZA, a chatbot mimicking a Rogerian psychotherapist. It demonstrated the illusion of understanding through pattern matching.

Read more
1974 – 1997

Winters & Experts

Funding dried up as promises fell short. The field pivoted to logic-based "Expert Systems" until Deep Blue shocked the world by defeating a chess grandmaster.

1980

Expert Systems Boom

XCON (eXpert CONfigurer) saves DEC millions. The industry shifts from general intelligence to specialized 'Expert Systems' that use if-then logic rules.

Read more
1986

Backpropagation

Hinton, Rumelhart, and Williams popularize backpropagation, allowing multi-layer neural networks to learn effectively, thawing the AI winter.

Read more
1997

Deep Blue vs. Kasparov

IBM's Deep Blue defeats reigning world chess champion Garry Kasparov. A watershed moment proving machines could out-calculate human intuition in complex games.

Read more
1997 – 2020

Deep Learning

Big data met GPU compute. Neural networks layered deeper than ever before, cracking vision (ImageNet), games (AlphaGo), and the architecture of language (Transformers).

2012

AlexNet / ImageNet

A deep convolutional neural network (AlexNet) crushes the competition at the ImageNet challenge, reducing error rates by half and igniting the modern Deep Learning explosion.

Read more
2016

AlphaGo

Google DeepMind's AlphaGo defeats Lee Sedol in Go, a game with more board configurations than atoms in the universe, using reinforcement learning.

Read more
2017

Attention Is All You Need

Google researchers introduce the Transformer architecture. This parallelization mechanism forms the backbone of all modern LLMs (GPT, Claude, Gemini).

Read more
2020 – 2024

Generative Explosion

AI learned to create. LLMs reached human parity on tests, art generators disrupted creativity, and ChatGPT brought AI into every home.

2020

GPT-3 Release

OpenAI releases GPT-3. With 175 billion parameters, it demonstrates few-shot learning, writing code, poetry, and prose with uncanny coherence.

Read more
2022

ChatGPT

OpenAI launches ChatGPT (GPT-3.5). It becomes the fastest-growing consumer app in history, reaching 100 million users in two months.

Read more
2024

Sora & Multimodality

AI masters video physics. Sora generates minute-long realistic video from text. GPT-4o and Gemini 1.5 Pro handle audio, vision, and text in real-time.

Read more
2025

Reasoning & Agents

The year AI began to think before speaking. Open reasoning models, massive context windows, and autonomous agents redefined the digital economy.

Jan 2025

DeepSeek-R1

DeepSeek shocks the industry by releasing R1, an open-source reasoning model that rivals proprietary giants at a fraction of the training cost. It introduces 'Chain of Thought' verification at scale.

Read more
Feb 2025

Grok-3 & Claude 3.7

A massive month. xAI's Grok-3 leverages real-time data from X to predict trends. Simultaneously, Anthropic releases Claude 3.7 Sonnet with significantly reduced hallucination rates.

Read more
Mar 2025

Gemini 2.5 Pro

Google strikes back with Gemini 2.5. It integrates deeply into the Android OS, allowing for 'Agentic Action'—booking flights and replying to emails autonomously.

Read more
May 2025

Claude 4 Opus

Anthropic releases the behemoth: Claude 4 Opus. It achieves near-perfect scores on graduate-level reasoning and exhibits subtle nuance in creative writing previously thought impossible.

Read more
Aug 2025

GPT-5 & MAI-1

OpenAI releases the long-awaited GPT-5, featuring 'System 2' thinking (slow, deliberate reasoning). Microsoft simultaneously launches MAI-1 to power enterprise reasoning clusters.

Read more
Sep 2025

Claude Sonnet 4.5

Anthropic optimizes the Opus architecture into Sonnet 4.5—delivering GPT-5 class performance at 1/10th the cost and speed, democratizing high-level reasoning for developers.

Read more
Nov 2025

The Agentic Era

OpenAI releases GPT-5.1 (o3 System) alongside Baidu's ERNIE 5.0. The focus shifts entirely from 'Chatbots' to 'Agents' that can execute complex, multi-step workflows autonomously over days.
